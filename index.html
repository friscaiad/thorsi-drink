About

The Coca-Cola Company is a multinational company from the United States in the beverage sector, including a manufacturer, retailer and marketer of non-alcoholic beverage concentrates and syrups, headquartered in Atlanta, Georgia, United States. Although Coca-Cola was invented and sold for the first time in 1888, the secret formula and Coca-Cola brand itself was only purchased in 1889 by Asa Griggs Candler, who incorporated it into The Coca-Cola Company three years later. On December 5, 1919, a consortium of entrepreneurs led by Ernest Woodruff, Robert W. Woodruff's father, bought The Coca-Cola Company for $ 25 million and immediately put it on the New York stock market.
